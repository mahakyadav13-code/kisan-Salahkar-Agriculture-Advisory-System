Hardware Setup Guide

1
Flash ESP32 Firmware

Open hardware/kisan_esp32_sensors.ino in Arduino IDE, set your WiFi SSID & password, and upload to ESP32.

2
Wire Sensors

DHT11 → GPIO 4  |  Soil Moisture → GPIO 34  |  pH Sensor → GPIO 35  |  Power via 18650 batteries.

3
Set Server IP

In the firmware, change SERVER_URL to your computer's IP address (e.g., http://192.168.1.100:5000).

4
Power On & Watch

Power on the ESP32. Data will appear here automatically every 30 seconds!

Wiring Diagram

┌──────────────┐
│    ESP32     │
│              │
│  GPIO 4  ────── DHT11 (DATA pin)
│  GPIO 34 ────── Soil Moisture (AO pin)
│  GPIO 35 ────── pH Sensor (AO pin)
│  3.3V    ────── Sensor VCC (all)
│  GND     ────── Sensor GND (all)
│  VIN     ────── 18650 Battery + (via holder)
│  GND     ────── 18650 Battery − (via holder)
└──────────────┘

DHT11: VCC → 3.3V | DATA → GPIO4 | GND → GND
Moisture: VCC → 3.3V | AO → GPIO34 | GND → GND
pH: VCC → 3.3V | AO → GPIO35 | GND → GND
Sensor Health Tip: Clean your soil moisture sensor every 2 weeks to avoid corrosion. DHT11 accuracy is ±2°C — calibrate against a mercury thermometer monthly for best results.

Ideal Sensor Ranges for Farming

When to take action

Temperature

20-35°C Ideal for most crops.
>38°C Heat stress alert.
<10°C Frost risk — cover seedlings.

Humidity

50-80% Optimal range.
>85% Fungal disease risk — improve ventilation.
<30% Increase irrigation frequency.

Soil Moisture

40-70% Healthy for most crops.
<20% Urgent irrigation needed.
>85% Waterlogging — improve drainage.

Soil pH

6.0-7.5 Ideal for most crops.
<5.5 Too acidic — add agricultural lime.
>8.0 Too alkaline — add gypsum/sulphur.
