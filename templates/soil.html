How to Get Soil Tested

  1. Visit your nearest Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) or Agriculture Department office
  2. Collect soil sample from 6-8 spots at 15cm depth, mix well
  3. Take about 500g sample in a clean bag with your details
  4. Submit to the lab — results in 2-3 weeks (FREE under Soil Health Card scheme)
  5. Enter your Soil Health Card number above to get recommendations
Healthy Soil = Healthy Crops: Get your soil tested every 2 years. The Soil Health Card scheme provides free testing at government labs. Call 1800-180-1551 (toll-free) for nearest center.

Understand Your Soil Nutrients

Ideal ranges for healthy crops

Nitrogen (N)

Ideal: 250-500 kg/ha — Essential for leaf growth. Low N causes yellowing. Add urea or green manure.

Phosphorus (P)

Ideal: 10-25 kg/ha — Needed for root development & flowering. Use DAP or bone meal for deficiency.

Potassium (K)

Ideal: 110-280 kg/ha — Strengthens stems & disease resistance. Apply MOP (Muriate of Potash) if low.

Soil pH

Ideal: 6.0-7.5 — Too acidic? Add lime. Too alkaline? Add gypsum or sulphur. Most crops prefer neutral pH.
